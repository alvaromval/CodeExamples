 Decision matrix:
Una matriz de decisión no es un instrumento de evaluación como tal. Es un instrumento que sirve para elegir una opción
entre una serie de opciones posibles, normalmente más de dos. Ponderando por igual todas.

A decision matrix is not an assessment instrument at all. Is an instrument used to choose an option between a list of them,
normally two or more.  Weigh them all.

Creating a decision matrix:

To create a decision matrix you have to follow the next steps:

An empty decision matrix is like this:


In the decision matrix we have three fields: title, numbero fo competences and actors:
- Title: the matrix title
- Nº competences: the number of optiones to weight, i.e, if we are weighting three cars, in order to choose one, each competence will be a car.
- Nº actors: The people who participates in the decision, everyone will have to weight each option.
The option (competence) which has been selected more, will win.

Let's see Peter results:
In the first cell he has selected 2 over 1, he chooses Fiat over Renault. He also has selected 1 over 3, he chooses Renault over Ford.
In the second cell he has selected 2 over 3, he chooses Fiat over Ford.

Let's see Mary results:
In the first cell she has selected 1 over 2, she chooses Renault over Fiat. She also has selected 3 over 1, she chooses Ford over Renault.
In the second cell she has selected 3 over 2, she chooses Ford over Fiat.

The last cell (in this case Ford's cell) will be always empty, because it will always weight in the previous cells.

To see the results, we have to press the button "Results" under the matrix:

Los resultados arrojan que ha habido un triple empate entre los tres coches (como se podía intuir en el momento de analizar las opciones elegidas) aunque podemos
ver como el actor A (Pedro) ha marcado marcado más veces la opción de Fiat, mientras que el actor B (María) ha marcado más Ford. Pero por contra como ambos han
elegido 1 vez Renault, ha habido un triple empate.

The results show a draw between the three competences, this is because the three has been selected the same times. But for Peter, wins competence number 2 (Fiat)
and for Mary wins competence number 3 (Ford), although the global results are the same for the three competences.
